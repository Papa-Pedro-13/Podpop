Our design

Design History

Design played a pivotal role in the development of our brand. We are dedicated to capturing the essence of Dutch history, drawing inspiration from the iconic Dutch De Stijl movement and the remarkable works of Piet Mondrian.

The De Stijl movement, which emerged over a century ago, embodies ideals of harmony, order, and universality.

Led by artists Theo van Doesburg and Piet Mondrian, it championed the reduction of form and color to their simplest, most essential elements, and emphasized geometric abstraction.

The idea of order and simplicity seeps into every aspect of podpop's concept and philosophy, both as a brand and in our distinct cushions.

Our colours

Taking inspiration from the timeless De Stijl, our color scheme is anchored by the primary colors - yellow, blue, and red - and nicely balanced by white, gray, and black. Each color choice is deliberate, designed to work harmoniously with the rest.

Our hero, however, is the yellow color. Bright and lively, yellow naturally seizes the spotlight, emanating energy and warmth.

It's no surprise that yellow is often linked with joy, sunlight, and optimism - same sentiments that resonate with our brand's vibe. We're all about crafting products that spread happiness and lift spirits.

Our vision

In our pursuit of balance and harmony, we incorporated the golden rectangle. This concept, with a ratio of around 1.618:1, is celebrated for its aesthetic allure.

This is precisely the reason why the design of our folded podpop adheres to this striking ratio.

Our logo

When crafting our logo, we placed great emphasis on achieving a sense of harmony and encouraging a more inclusive and welcoming feel.

Our effortlessly recognizable "podpop" logo has sleek edges and is accented with a bold yellow stripe. It symbolizes out product itself, subtly mirroring its characteristics and attributes.

Where does podpop come from?

The concept behind the name "podpop" for our product emerged from the combination of two elements: "pod," reflecting the concept of our cushion as a small space for relaxation, and "pop," indicating something bright, popular, and attention-grabbing.

Thus, "podpop" implies a comfortable and stylish seating area that we have created.
